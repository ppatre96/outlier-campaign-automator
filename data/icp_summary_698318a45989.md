## 5. Targeting takeaways for an agency

- **Primary skill anchors:** Python, JavaScript, React — require LinkedIn targeting to include at least one.
- **Degree floor:** Bachelors (and above).
- **Most represented field:** Computer Science.
- **Strongest combo to A/B test:** `Python + JavaScript` (31 activators / 13%).
- **Geography:** Global, minus platform-blocked countries (per `PROJECT_QUALIFICATIONS_LONG`).
- **Exclusions:** Sales / Staffing / Recruiting titles and skills (hurt conversion).
- **Employer prestige:** 2% at top-tier global cos, 1% at top-tier in-country cos. LinkedIn `currentCompanies` targeting using these names as a positive-signal boost is a cheap win; most activators are at mid-tier / smaller firms so keep this as a bonus layer, not a hard filter.
- **Underperforming employer segment:** 5% at lower-tier IT-services firms (TCS / Infosys / Wipro / Cognizant / etc.) — consider excluding these via LinkedIn `currentCompanies` exclude facet. Activators from these companies exist but are under-represented relative to their employer share of the Indian software workforce.
- **University prestige:** 17% of activators attended a top-tier university (strongest: Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati, Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur). Use as an `schools` facet signal on LinkedIn for an anchored-combos cohort.
