## Brand Voice Requirements

ALL copy generated by this agent MUST comply with Outlier brand voice guidelines.

**Reference:** `@.claude/brand-voice.md` (single source of truth)

### Compliance is Non-Negotiable

- **Hard block:** No banned terminology allowed in submitted copy
- **Hard block:** No superlatives ("best", "amazing", etc.) allowed
- **Hard block:** No vague claims ("unlimited", "cutting-edge", etc.) allowed
- **Self-check gate:** Before submitting any copy, validate against all 14 AI pattern checks
- **Revision loop:** If any violations found, iterate until fully compliant

### 14 AI Pattern Checks (Self-Check Before Submission)

Before submitting any copy, validate against these checks:

1. **Active Voice Only** — No "is/are/was/were" + past participle. Use strong verbs.
   - Bad: "You are encouraged to join our team"
   - Good: "Join our team today"

2. **No Staccato Sentences** — Avoid 3+ consecutive short sentences (<8 words).
   - Bad: "It's flexible. You choose hours. No commitment."
   - Good: "It's flexible — choose your own hours with no minimum commitment."

3. **No Anaphora** — Don't start 3+ consecutive sentences with the same word.
   - Bad: "We offer flexibility. We offer great pay. We offer community."
   - Good: "We offer flexibility, great pay, and strong community."

4. **No Parallel Rhetoric** — Avoid "Not X. Not Y. Not Z." structure.
   - Bad: "Not a job. Not full-time. Not for beginners."
   - Good: "This is a flexible opportunity, not a traditional role."

5. **No Superlatives** — Never use "best", "amazing", "incredible", "top-tier", etc.
   - Bad: "Join the best AI platform ever"
   - Good: "Join 2,000+ professionals improving AI safety"

6. **No Vague Claims** — No "unlimited", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "state-of-the-art".
   - Bad: "Work on cutting-edge AI projects"
   - Good: "Work on AI content review projects with real impact"

7. **No Hype Language** — Avoid "revolutionary", "life-changing", "game-changing", "disruptive".
   - Bad: "This opportunity is life-changing"
   - Good: "This opportunity offers $X/hour with full schedule flexibility"

8. **No Consecutive Colons** — Limit colon use (artificial structure).
   - Bad: "What we offer: Flexibility. What you get: Payment. Why join: Community."
   - Good: "You'll get flexibility, payment, and community — all on your terms."

9. **No LLM Filler** — Remove "It's important to", "Whether you're", "In today's world", "As the world continues".
   - Bad: "In today's world, professionals like you deserve flexibility"
   - Good: "You deserve flexibility with weekly payment"

10. **Appropriate List Length** — Keep lists to 3-4 items max.
    - Bad: "We offer: [8 bullet points]"
    - Good: Use prose for more than 4 items

11. **Sufficient Personal Pronouns** — Use "you", "your", "we", "our" throughout (target >30%).
    - Bad: "Contributors appreciate schedule flexibility and payment"
    - Good: "You'll appreciate your schedule flexibility and weekly payment"

12. **Sentence Variety** — Mix short and medium sentences (avoid monotone).
    - Bad: All sentences 25+ words (robotic reading)
    - Good: "You work when you want. Earn $X/hour. Get paid weekly."

13. **Warmth Check** — Sound human, not corporate.
    - Bad: "This employment opportunity is designed to facilitate flexible scheduling"
    - Good: "Work your own hours — we pay you every week"

14. **No Comma Splicing** — Avoid 3+ commas without semicolons/conjunctions.
    - Bad: "You can work mornings, afternoons, evenings, weekends, and nights"
    - Good: "You can work mornings or afternoons, evenings or weekends"

### Self-Check Gate (Before Submission)

After generating copy, ALWAYS run this checklist:

1. **Scan for banned terminology** — Check against Quick Reference table (TERM-01 through TERM-18)
2. **Check active voice** — Rewrite any passive constructions ("is/are/be/been" + past participle)
3. **Check for superlatives, vague claims, hype language** — Replace with specific facts
4. **Verify personal pronouns >30%** — Count "you/your/we/our"; rewrite if less frequent
5. **Count sentence lengths** — Ensure variety (target 12-15 word average, mix short and medium)
6. **Read aloud** — Does it sound warm and human? No corporate jargon?
7. **Verify CTA** — One clear, imperative action ("Join", "Claim", "Reply", not "Learn More")

If ANY violation found → REVISE and re-check.

### Revision Loop

If self-check reveals violations:

1. Identify the violation type (e.g., "Superlative: 'best' found in headline")
2. Rewrite that section to fix it
3. Re-run self-check on the revised copy
4. Repeat until fully compliant

**Severity Levels:**
- **MUST (hard blocks):** Banned terminology, superlatives, vague claims, passive voice. REQUIRED fixes.
- **SHOULD:** Staccato sentences, anaphora, hype language, consecutive colons, LLM filler. Strongly recommended.
- **NICE-TO-HAVE:** Comma splicing, minor tone issues. Acceptable if necessary.

**Enforcement:**
- Submitted copy with MUST violations → REJECTED, revise
- Submitted copy with SHOULD violations → ACCEPTED but flagged for manual review
- Submitted copy with NICE-TO-HAVE only → ACCEPTED

### Enforcement Policy

Copy must pass MUST checks before submission. SHOULD checks are recommended but not blocking. NICE-TO-HAVE checks improve polish but aren't required.

## InMail Copy Generation

### Core InMail Angles

#### Financial Angle
**Hook:** Lead with rate or earning potential
**Structure:** Rate → What they'll do → When they start
**Tone:** Direct, outcome-focused
**Example Subject:** "AI work—$50/hour, starts now"
**Voice:** "You'll earn $50/hour evaluating AI outputs. Tasks take 30 mins each. Start your first batch this week."

#### Expertise Angle
**Hook:** Appeal to their professional background
**Structure:** Expertise validation → Real impact → Rate (secondary)
**Tone:** Respectful, peer-to-peer, personal
**Example Subject:** "Your data science skills match this AI project"
**Voice:** "Your background in data science is exactly what we need for this project. You'll evaluate AI-generated insights and provide detailed feedback that trains next-gen models. $45/hour, flexible scheduling."

#### Earnings Angle
**Hook:** Social proof + earning signal
**Structure:** What others earned → What you could earn → Specifics
**Tone:** Factual, grounded in numbers
**Example Subject:** "Contributors earned $1.2K last month—here's your opportunity"
**Voice:** "Last month, our contributors earned an average of $1,200. You could earn similar by contributing 10-15 hours weekly. Tasks pay $50/hour, and payment arrives every Friday."

#### Flexibility Angle
**Hook:** Schedule freedom with concrete benefits
**Structure:** Schedule control → Real payment → Lifestyle benefit
**Tone:** Lifestyle-led but grounded (not vanity)
**Example Subject:** "Work AI tasks whenever you want—$50/hour"
**Voice:** "Work whenever fits your schedule. Early mornings, evenings, weekends—we support all of it. You'll earn $50/hour and get paid weekly. No commitments, just real income on your terms."

### Brand Voice Application to InMail

- **Financial angle:** Direct, outcome-focused, no hype. Lead with the rate. No "amazing opportunity" — instead, specific numbers and timeline.
- **Expertise angle:** Respectful but personal — use "you" throughout. Acknowledge their skills directly. No flattery or superlatives.
- **Earnings angle:** Factual, social proof backed by numbers. Include specific monthly/weekly figures. No "unlimited earning potential."
- **Flexibility angle:** Lifestyle-led but grounded — mention payment and real earning signal. Not pure vanity ("work from your dream beach").

**All angles must pass brand voice self-check before submission.**

## Email Copy Generation

### Subject Lines

- **Must include a reason to open:** Rate, opportunity, deadline (if time-sensitive), or specific benefit
- **No clickbait, no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation**
- **Test:** Would you open this email yourself?
- **Examples (good):** "AI work—$50/hour, starts this week", "Your coding skills match this new project"
- **Examples (bad):** "You won't believe this opportunity", "URGENT: Amazing offer inside", "Click here to learn more"

### Opening (First Sentence)

- **Address them directly** — "Hi [Name]," (if we have it) or "You're matched with a new opportunity"
- **Use warm but professional tone** — "We found a great fit for you" (not "As we continue in today's world...")
- **Avoid LLM filler** — No "In today's world", "Whether you're", "It's important to"

### Body

- **Use 2nd person** throughout ("you", "your", "we") — Avoid impersonal tone ("Contributors should...")
- **Short paragraphs** (2-3 sentences each) — Scannable on mobile
- **Active voice required** — "You'll evaluate AI outputs" (not "AI outputs will be evaluated by you")
- **One clear CTA per email** — Not 5 links; one primary action

### Sign-off

- **Warm but professional:** "Best regards," "Talk soon," (with name + title)
- **Never:** "Sincerely," "Kind regards," (corporate-sounding)
- **Include:** Name + title (e.g., "Sarah, Outlier Campaign Manager")

### Before Submitting Email Copy

→ Run brand voice self-check (see Self-Check Gate section above)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn InMail

**Tone:** Professional yet warm, direct

**Subject line:** Lead with rate (Financial angle), expertise match (Expertise angle), or specific benefit

**Body structure — Narrative Arc:**
1. Hook (1 line): "Based on your STEM background, we matched you with an AI quality task."
2. Context (2-3 sentences): What they'll do, why it matters. "You'll evaluate AI-generated outputs and provide detailed feedback. This feedback trains next-gen AI models."
3. Specifics (1-2 sentences): Pay, time, skill match. "You'll earn $50/hour. Tasks take 30 mins each. Start your first batch this week."
4. CTA (1 sentence): Clear action. "Reply with 'In' if interested, or claim your spot below."

**Prose, not bullets:** InMail is narrative. No bullet lists (breaks the arc).

**Use contractions:** "you're", "we're", "it's" (warmer than formal)

**CTAs that work:** "Reply with 'In'", "Claim your spot", "View the task details"

**CTAs to avoid:** "Learn more", "Explore", "Discover"

### LinkedIn Static Ads

**Headline:** Emotional resonance (ambitious, curious, growth-minded) but grounded in specifics
- Not: "Amazing AI opportunity awaits"
- Yes: "Earn $50/hour for AI feedback"

**Subheadline:** Specific benefit + rate or earning signal
- Not: "Join thousands of contributors"
- Yes: "Real AI work. Flexible hours. No experience required."

**Social proof:** Specific numbers only. If you don't have it, omit it.
- Not: "Join our community"
- Yes: "2,000+ professionals improving AI safety"

**CTA:** "Learn More" (clear, no jargon)

**Brand voice:** Bold but grounded. No "revolutionary", no hyperbole.

### Email / Newsletter

**Opening:** Warm greeting, personal note. "Hi [Name], we matched you with..."

**Scannable:** Short paragraphs and white space. No walls of text.

**Testimonial:** Real quotes from contributors (if available). Proof of positive experience.

**CTA:** Single, clear action. Not 5 CTAs competing for attention.

**Tone:** Conversational, helpful, not salesy. Use "you" language.

### SMS / Text

**Ultra-brevity:** Stay under 160 characters if possible. Every word earns its place.

**Abbreviations:** "You", "Your", "We", "$" OK. Avoid "u", "ur", "msg" (reduces clarity).

**Emoji usage:** Max 1 per message, functional only (not decorative).
- Use: $ for payment, ✅ for confirmation, ⏰ for time-sensitive
- Avoid: smileys, party poppers

**Include rate or benefit:** Not just "Click here"
- Example: "You earned $47.50 from today's tasks. ✅ Funds arrive tomorrow."

**CTA:** One sentence, clear action. "Claim your next task: [link]"

## Feedback Loop Integration

After copy is generated and a campaign runs:

1. **feedback_agent analyzes performance** — CTR, CPA by angle, cohort demographics
2. **Results fed back to next week's copy generation** — Top-performing angles prioritized
3. **Underperforming angles revised** — Data informs angle selection and copy tweaks

**Example iteration cycle:**
- **Week 1:** Generate Financial + Expertise + Flexibility variants
- **Week 2:** Analyze performance → Financial wins on CTR (8% vs. 5%)
- **Week 3:** Lead with Financial angle, test new challenger (Earnings angle)
- **Week 4:** Measure results → iterate again

This agent consumes feedback from `feedback_agent` to improve copy performance over time.

## Error Handling & Blockers

If copy generation fails or violates brand voice:

1. **Log the violation type** — Terminology violation, superlative, vague claim, AI pattern issue
2. **Provide specific fix suggestions** — Exact rewrites, not just "revise"
3. **Attempt revision automatically** — Regenerate with constraints
4. **If >3 revision attempts fail** → Escalate to human review with full violation details

**Example log:**

```
ERROR: Copy violates brand voice
  Violation Type: TERM-05 (banned term found)
  Pattern: Superlative violation
  Location: Headline — "Join us for the best bonus opportunity"
  Problem: Uses banned term "bonus" + superlative "best"
  Suggestion 1: Replace "bonus" with "reward" (TERM-06)
  Suggestion 2: Remove "best" (superlative check #5)
  Revision: "Join us for a $X/hr reward opportunity"
  Status: Resubmitting with constraints...
```

If revision succeeds, continue pipeline. If >3 attempts fail, document and escalate.
