## Decisions Made

- **scipy.stats.entropy direct, no wrapping:** `entropy(p, q)` IS KL when `q` is provided (relative entropy). No need for a manual `sum(p*log(p/q))` and no need for a normalizing wrapper since we pre-normalize after EPSILON addition for explicit zero-bin handling.
- **EPSILON applied to BOTH vectors, not just one:** RESEARCH-V2 Pitfall 4 stipulates this — a zero in `p` while `q>0` is fine for KL, but a zero in `q` while `p>0` produces inf. Applying EPSILON to both is the safe default.
- **Strict `<` rate-limit comparison:** `now - last_ts < timedelta(days=7)` means a trigger exactly 7 days after the previous one is allowed (boundary test verifies this). This matches the user's "at most one per 7 days" framing rather than ">7 days strictly".
- **_invoke_trigger handles both shapes:** Current `ReanalysisOrchestrator.trigger_reanalysis` (src/reanalysis_loop.py:16-26) is sync. v1 Plan 04 SUMMARY mentions a sync wrapper; the underlying may go async later. `inspect.iscoroutine` keeps the monitor portable across both contracts.
- **Cold-start cutoff at 2 (not 4) snapshots:** Plan explicitly states "fewer than 2 weeks of snapshots ⇒ skip" via the constraints block. Baseline aggregates last (up to) 4 snapshots; 2-3 still produces a baseline.
- **snapshot() never raises:** Wrapped Redash fetch in try/except so a 502 from Redash does not break the weekly cron — empty parquet is written and cold-start path handles it next week.
