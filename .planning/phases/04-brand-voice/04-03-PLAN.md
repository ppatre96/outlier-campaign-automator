## Brand Voice Requirements

ALL copy generated by this agent MUST comply with Outlier brand voice guidelines.
Reference: `@.claude/brand-voice.md` (single source of truth)

### Compliance is Non-Negotiable
- Hard block: No banned terminology allowed in submitted copy
- Hard block: No superlatives ("best", "amazing", etc.) allowed
- Hard block: No vague claims ("unlimited", "cutting-edge", etc.) allowed
- Self-check gate: Before submitting any copy, validate against all 14 AI pattern checks

### Quick Terminology Reference
| Don't Say | Instead, Say | Platform |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Job | Task, opportunity | All |
| Required | Strongly encouraged | All |
| Role, position | Opportunity | All |
| Compensation | Payment | All |
| Training, learning | Become familiar with project guidelines | Email, InMail |
| Bonus | Reward | All |
| Assign | Match | InMail |
| Team | Part of this project / member of this group | Community, Email |
| Instructions | Project guidelines | All |
| Performance | Progress | Monitoring |
| ... | ... | ... |
*Full list: see @.claude/brand-voice.md*

### 14 AI Pattern Checks (Self-Check Before Submission)

Before submitting any copy:

1. **Active Voice Only** — No "is/are/was/were" + past participle. Use strong verbs.
   - Bad: "You are encouraged to join our team"
   - Good: "Join our team today"

2. **No Staccato Sentences** — Avoid 3+ consecutive short sentences (<8 words).
   - Bad: "It's flexible. You choose hours. No commitment."
   - Good: "It's flexible — choose your own hours with no minimum commitment."

3. **No Anaphora** — Don't start 3+ consecutive sentences with the same word.
   - Bad: "We offer flexibility. We offer great pay. We offer community."
   - Good: "We offer flexibility, great pay, and strong community."

4. **No Parallel Rhetoric** — Avoid "Not X. Not Y. Not Z." structure.
   - Bad: "Not a job. Not full-time. Not for beginners."
   - Good: "This is a flexible opportunity, not a traditional role."

5. **No Superlatives** — Never use "best", "amazing", "incredible", "top-tier", etc.
   - Bad: "Join the best AI platform ever"
   - Good: "Join 2,000+ professionals improving AI safety"

6. **No Vague Claims** — No "unlimited", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "state-of-the-art".
   - Bad: "Work on cutting-edge AI projects"
   - Good: "Work on AI content review projects with real impact"

7. **No Hype Language** — Avoid "revolutionary", "life-changing", "game-changing", "disruptive".
   - Bad: "This opportunity is life-changing"
   - Good: "This opportunity offers $X/hour with full schedule flexibility"

8. **No Consecutive Colons** — Limit colon use (artificial structure).
   - Bad: "What we offer: Flexibility. What you get: Payment. Why join: Community."
   - Good: "You'll get flexibility, payment, and community — all on your terms."

9. **No LLM Filler** — Remove "It's important to", "Whether you're", "In today's world", "As the world continues".
   - Bad: "In today's world, professionals like you deserve flexibility"
   - Good: "You deserve flexibility with weekly payment"

10. **Appropriate List Length** — Keep lists to 3-4 items max.
    - Bad: "We offer: [8 bullet points]"
    - Good: Use prose for more than 4 items

11. **Sufficient Personal Pronouns** — Use "you", "your", "we", "our" throughout.
    - Bad: "Contributors appreciate schedule flexibility and payment"
    - Good: "You'll appreciate your schedule flexibility and weekly payment"

12. **Sentence Variety** — Mix short and medium sentences (avoid monotone).
    - Bad: All sentences 25+ words (robotic reading)
    - Good: "You work when you want. Earn $X/hour. Get paid weekly."

13. **Warmth Check** — Sound human, not corporate.
    - Bad: "This employment opportunity is designed to facilitate flexible scheduling"
    - Good: "Work your own hours — we pay you every week"

14. **No Comma Splicing** — Avoid 3+ commas without semicolons/conjunctions.
    - Bad: "You can work mornings, afternoons, evenings, weekends, and nights"
    - Good: "You can work mornings or afternoons, evenings or weekends"

### Self-Check Gate (Before Submission)

After generating copy, ALWAYS:
1. Scan for banned terminology (see Quick Reference table)
2. Check active voice — rewrite passive constructions
3. Check for superlatives, vague claims, hype language
4. Verify personal pronouns >30% of copy
5. Count sentence lengths — ensure variety
6. Read aloud — does it sound warm and human?

If ANY violation found → REVISE and re-check.

### Revision Loop

If self-check reveals violations:
1. Identify the violation type (e.g., "superlative: 'best'")
2. Rewrite that section to fix it
3. Re-run self-check on the revised copy
4. Repeat until fully compliant (0 hard blocks: MUST severity)

MUST violations (hard blocks): Hard-block fixes required
SHOULD violations: Strongly recommended fixes
NICE_TO_HAVE violations: Acceptable if necessary

### Enforcement Policy

- Submitted copy with MUST violations → REJECTED, agent must revise
- Submitted copy with SHOULD violations → ACCEPTED but flagged for manual review
- Submitted copy with NICE_TO_HAVE only → ACCEPTED

```markdown
## Email Copy Generation

Subject lines:
- Must include a reason to open (rate, opportunity, deadline if time-sensitive)
- No clickbait, no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation
- Test: Would you open this email yourself?

Opening (first sentence):
- Address them directly ("Hi [Name]")
- Use warm but professional tone
- Avoid LLM filler ("In today's world", "Whether you're")

Body:
- Use 2nd person ("you", "your") throughout
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences each)
- Active voice required
- One clear CTA per email

Sign-off:
- "Best regards," or "Talk soon," (warm, not formal)
- Include name + title
- No "Sincerely," or "Kind regards," (corporate-sounding)

**Before submitting email copy → run brand voice self-check**
```

**4. Platform-Specific Notes:**

```markdown
## Platform-Specific Notes

### LinkedIn InMail
- Tone: Professional yet warm, direct
- Subject line: Lead with rate for Financial angle, expertise for Expertise angle
- Body: Narrative arc — hook → context → benefit → CTA
- No bullet lists in InMail (it's prose)
- Use contractions ("you're", "we're") for warmth

### LinkedIn Static Ads
- Headline: Emotional resonance (ambitious, curious, growth-minded)
- Subheadline: Specific benefit + rate or earning signal
- Social proof: "2,000+ professionals" or specific cohort stat
- CTA: "Learn More" (no jargon)
- Brand voice: Bold but grounded (no "revolutionary", no hyperbole)

### Email / Newsletter
- Opening: Warm greeting, personal note
- Scannable: Use short paragraphs and white space
- Testimonial: Real quotes from contributors (if available)
- CTA: Single, clear action (not 5 CTAs)
- Tone: Conversational, helpful, not salesy

### SMS / Text
- Ultra-brevity but stay warm
- Use "you" even in limited space
- Include rate or benefit (not just "Click here")
- No abbreviations except standard (USD, etc.)
- CTA in one sentence
```

## Brand Voice Alignment

The creative briefs generated by this agent inform copy generation by downstream agents (outlier-copy-writer, figma-creative). All briefs MUST be consistent with Outlier brand voice guidelines.

Reference: `@.claude/brand-voice.md` (full guidelines)

### Brand Voice Constraints on Brief Output

When generating a brief, ensure:

1. **Tone Specification**: Output must explicitly state the tone for the brief:
   - "direct and outcome-focused" (not "professional and inspiring")
   - "warm and personal" (not "friendly and engaging")
   - "respectful and peer-to-peer" (not "empowering and motivational")

2. **Headline/Subheadline Generation**:
   - Never use superlatives ("best", "amazing", "incredible")
   - Never use vague claims ("unlimited", "cutting-edge", "world-class")
   - Include specific benefit (rate, flexibility, expertise required)
   - Use active voice ("Join X contributors" not "Be part of our community")

3. **Photo Subject Specifications**:
   - Describe real, grounded scenarios (not "inspiring" or "aspirational" alone)
   - Bad: "aspirational professional in modern workspace"
   - Good: "person at desk with laptop, focused on work"

4. **CTA Specification**:
   - Use clear, direct CTAs: "Learn More", "Start Today", "Apply Now"
   - No vague CTAs: "Discover", "Explore", "Find Your Path"
   - Avoid corporate jargon: "Engage with", "Connect with", "Leverage"

5. **Experiment Field**:
   - If test variant, must specify the angle (Financial, Expertise, Earnings, Flexibility)
   - Include photo_subject variation that differs from baseline
   - Ensure test copy adheres to brand voice (no extra superlatives in tests)

### Sample Output with Brand Voice

```json
{
  "cohort": "DATA_ANALYST",
  "headline": "Data Analysts | $40/hr + Flexible Schedule",
  "subheadline": "Join 500+ analysts improving AI — work whenever you want",
  "photo_subject": "person_at_desk_laptop_focused",
  "angle_mood": "direct, outcome-focused",
  "cta": "Start Today",
  "experiment": false,
  "tone": "direct and factual"
}
```

Notice:
- Headline includes rate (specific benefit)
- Subheadline has specific number ("500+"), not vague claim
- Photo subject is concrete, not emotional
- Tone is explicitly stated
- CTA is clear and direct

### Brand Voice Violations to Avoid

This generator should NOT output:
- Superlatives in headlines ("the best opportunity")
- Vague claims in subheadlines ("cutting-edge AI work")
- Emotional manipulation in photo subject ("empowering professional")
- Weak CTAs ("Learn More About This Amazing Opportunity")
- Corporate filler in tone ("innovative and forward-thinking")

If you find yourself outputting these, revise the brief to ground it in specific facts (rate, schedule, impact) instead of emotional appeal.
