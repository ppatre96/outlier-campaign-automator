**4. 14 AI Pattern Checks Section:**
Document each check as a testable pattern. For each, include:
  - Check name (e.g., "Active Voice")
  - Rule description
  - What to flag (examples of violations)
  - Why it matters (e.g., "passive voice waters down agency")
  - Detection method (regex or heuristic)

The 14 checks:
1. **Active Voice Only** — Flag "is/are/be" followed by past participles (e.g., "is designed", "are created"). Promote action verbs.
2. **No Staccato Sentences** — Flag patterns of 3+ sentences <8 words. Staccato reads robotic/AI-like.
3. **No Anaphora (repetitive sentence openings)** — Flag when 3+ consecutive sentences start with same word/phrase.
4. **No Parallel Rhetoric** — Flag patterns like "Not X. Not Y. Not Z." (common LLM artifact).
5. **No Superlatives** — Flag "best", "amazing", "incredible", "the most", "top-tier", etc.
6. **No Vague Claims** — Flag "unlimited", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "state-of-the-art", "next-gen".
7. **No Hype Language** — Flag "revolutionary", "life-changing", "once-in-a-lifetime", "game-changing", "disruptive".
8. **No Consecutive Colons** — Flag use of colons for artificial structure (common in AI copy).
9. **No LLM Filler** — Flag "It's important to", "Whether you're", "In today's world", "As the world continues".
10. **No Lists Without Reason** — Flag bullet lists >4 items in email/InMail (suggests lazy structure, not natural flow).
11. **Personal Pronouns Requirement** — Flag copy with <30% personal pronouns (you, we, your, our). Impersonal tone.
12. **Sentence Variety** — Flag when avg sentence length is 20+ words OR <8 words (monotonous rhythm).
13. **Warmth Check** — Flag copy that sounds corporate/robotic (too many gerunds, passive constructions, no contractions).
14. **No Comma Splicing** — Flag 3+ commas in a single sentence without semicolons/conjunctions.

For each check, document:
- Severity (MUST, SHOULD, NICE-TO-HAVE)
- Example violation
- How to fix it
